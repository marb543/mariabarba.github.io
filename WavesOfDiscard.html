ABOUT THE PROJECT


Similar to the sea's natural tides, the Waves of Discard explores the unending cycles of consumerism. This piece of art explores society's fixation with the "new," exposing its costs to both human culture and the environment, with the sea being particularly affected. The fast-paced online trend cycles, in which new technology is momentarily celebrated before being abandoned and forgotten, serve as the inspiration for this piece.

EXHIBITION


Five distinct videos, each lasting 1:30 to 2:00 minutes, make up the project's video component and play continuously throughout the exhibition. We have selected models for the videos who represent a diverse and inclusive vision for the exhibition, regardless of their gender, ethnicity, or physical characteristics.

The primary concept behind the videos is that our models are sirens, each screen portraying a distinct interpretation of siren mythology through the lens of fashion. The videos will open with the sirens' captivating gaze, gradually transitioning to unsettling and provocative imagery, designed to "lure" exhibition visitors with ethereal, fashion-forward visuals.

This juxtaposition aims to evoke discomfort and spark critical reflection. To underscore their connection with the discarded nature of fast fashion, the sirens will interact with garments and accessories made from repurposed materials, such as shredded fabrics, outdated styles, and textiles adorned with remnants of obsolete technology like wires, CDs, and headphones.

The unsettling atmosphere is enhanced by the audio track, which unifies the overall experience across the CTR TVs.
